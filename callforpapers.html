Overview

The 21st Asia Joint Conference on Information Security (AsiaJCIS 2026) will be held in Guangdong, China on August 4–5, 2026, hosted by Guangdong University of Science and Technology (GDUST).

This conference focuses on the technical and practical aspects of security applications and will serve as a forum to present new results from the academic research community and industry. Original papers are solicited for submission to AsiaJCIS 2026. Distinguished security experts will also be invited to give keynote and technical talks.

Conference website: https://easychair.org/cfp/AsiaJCIS2026

Topics include, but are not limited to:

Cryptography

Post-Quantum Cryptography, Quantum Cryptography, Applied Cryptography, Cryptanalysis, Side Channel Attacks, Blockchain & Smart Contract Security

Cloud Computing Security, NGN Security, Smart Home, Mobile Security, VANET, DoS/DDoS Countermeasures, 5G/6G Security, Zero Trust Architecture

System Security

Computer/Application Security, VM Security, Database Security, Secure eHealth/eCommerce, IoT, Ubiquitous Computing, Industrial Control System Security

Email Security, Web Services, SNS, Mobile Security, IPTV, Software Supply Chain Security, AI Security & Privacy

Security Management, Big Data Security, Trusted Computing, Data Breach, Intrusion Detection, Phishing, Privacy-Enhancing Technology

AI & Emerging Threats

Smart Grid Security, Adversarial Machine Learning, LLM Security, Biometrics, Usable Security & Privacy, Misinformation/Fake News Detection

Instructions for Authors

  • Authors are invited to submit original papers. Papers must not substantially duplicate work that any authors have published elsewhere or submitted in parallel to other conferences.
  • This conference adopts double-blind peer review. Submitted manuscripts must be anonymous, with no author names, affiliations, acknowledgements, or obvious references to prior work.
  • Authors of accepted papers MUST give an oral presentation at the conference. Papers of authors who do not attend will be removed from proceedings and announced as no-show papers.
  • We accept both regular papers and short papers. The program committee will decide the format upon acceptance.
  • Submissions must be written in English and at most 8 pages long (including references and appendices) in IEEE Xplore format. Extra payment is required for additional pages.
  • Formatted to US Letter (8.5×11 in), two-column IEEE format, single-spaced 10-point Times fully justified text.
  • Submissions ignoring these guidelines may be rejected without review.
